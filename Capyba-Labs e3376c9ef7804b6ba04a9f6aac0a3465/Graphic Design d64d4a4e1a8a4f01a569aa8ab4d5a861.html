Graphic Design

In order to give some semblent of life to a game, one must not forget the importance of graphical design. In this vast domain of expertise, our first step was to create the main character: Camille Fonsal.

This is the first character design we did and its evolution. To keep it simple yet still detailed, we quickly decided that our charater would be in a 32 x 32 pixel format. As you may observe, a blue scarf was added to our original character, in order to make it a bit less sinister and more comical.

Further down the line, though we have used copyright free tilesets for testing purposes, we needed to our own tilesets that would be used in LDTK software. As we have 4 types of levels, we needed a forest tileset, a water/ice tileset, a sand desert tileset and finally, to add a touch of nonsense, a bubble gum tileset. The format is the same for each one of these, only the colors changes. Each tileset is a group of 32x32 square that lets us create pretty much everything we want for a basic level.

The tilesets come of course with a specific background. These are key to set a mood for the level. Is it dark? Is it dreamy?
