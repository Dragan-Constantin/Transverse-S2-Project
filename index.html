About

Project Horizon is a 2D indie platformer game available on itch.io, Steam and the Epic Store. Built using the Godot Engine, it was originally created as a school project at EFREI Paris engineering school in 2023.

In this game, players take control of the main character, a powerful alchemist named Camille Fonsal. After an unfortunate incident in his lab, he finds himself teleported to another world through a portal created by a mix of his potions and spells.

Players must help the alchemist on his journey to return home by completing quests to collect ancient artifacts. Along the way, he will encounter a druid who will guide him through various landscapes and environments, to face challenges and enemies alike.

As a platformer game, players will need to navigate through obstacles and enemies, using a ballista to launch themselves into the air and reach new areas of each level. With unique game mechanics and engaging gameplay, Project Horizon is a must-play for fans of indie platformer games.

The Team

Constantin Dragan is a multi-talented member of the team, serving as the project manager, original music composer, sound designer and map designer. He first and foremost oversees the development process and ensures that the game's audio and visual elements are top-notch. He is also the composer of all the game OSTs and created most of the sound effects.

Enora L'Azou specializes in creating the game's characters and tilesets, as well as contributing to map design. She brings her artistic flair and attention to detail to create engaging and immersive game environments. Everything you see began its life under her pen.

Maxime Cerruti is the lead programmer and co-project manager. He leads the technical development of the game and ensures that the programming team meets deadlines and achieves the project's objectives. He works alongside Constantin to oversee the development process and to ensure that all the game’s aspects are top-notch.

Harold Lajous is a game developer and designer who also contributes to map design. He uses his creative vision and technical expertise to design and implement game mechanics and features. Through time, he became our lead bughunter, for no bug can escape him for too long.

Charles Garbus is a game developer who takes on the role of marketing manager. He ensures that the game reaches its target audience through effective promotion and advertising strategies.

Amar-Adam Benadjaoud is a game developer and designer who also contributes to map design. He works closely with the programming team to create engaging game mechanics and gameplay features while ensuring a smooth and enjoyable gaming experience.

Amar Adam Benadjaoud:

During this project, I have acquired new skills such as the basics of GDScript programming and level design.

Indeed, throughout the project, I took on the task of finding the trajectory equation that we would later use in our game's main mechanic, and identifying the factors on which the player could have an influence.

Then, together with our lead developer, we implemented this trajectory equation into the game, which was coded in GDScript, a proprietary programming language I had never heard of before.

Since this task was completed quite early in the project’s time-table, I had to find a new way to be useful within the group. Therefore, I learned to use Level Design tool-kit (LDtk), a software for designing video game levels based on tilesets. Thanks to Enora's designs, I started creating the levels that would be used.

However, towards then end, a problem arose. Although we started the project on Godot 3.5, which had a perfectly working add-on allowing the import of levels created with LDtk, we ported our game from Godot 3.5 to the brand new and freshly released Godot 4.0. Unfortunately, it took some time for us to find a new add-on to import LDtk maps on Godot 4.0 and once we found one, we never got it to work properly.

Thus, I had to learn to use the level editor included in the software in order to provide you with playable levels. Ultimately, I am quite satisfied with my involvement in this project. However, I realized that there was a certain gap between my peers and I in several areas, such as design or programming.

One of my resolutions for this summer is to learn at least one new programming language that will allow me to expand my possibilities in programming.
